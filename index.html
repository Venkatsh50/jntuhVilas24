VILAS'24 A MEMMORIAL SPORTS EVENT

Every year, the Department of Electrical and Electronics Engineering at JNTUH University hosts the Vilas Memorial Sports Event in memory of our late senior, Sri Ram Vilas. Sri Ram Vilas passed away from bone cancer at a young age, but his passion for life and sports continues to inspire us

The Vilas Memorial Sports Event is a two-day event that features a variety of sports activities, including cricket, football, volleyball, and basketball. The event is open to all students, faculty, and staff at [University Name], and it is a great way to get involved in the community and learn more about cancer awareness and blood donation

In addition to the sports activities, the Vilas Memorial Sports Event also features a number of other events, such as a blood donation camp, an orphanage donation drive, and a cultural evening. The cultural evening is a special event that features music, dance, and other performances by students and faculty.

    CANCER AWARNESS

    Cancer is a leading cause of death worldwide, and it is important to be aware of the signs and symptoms of cancer. Early detection and treatment are essential for improving survival rates. The Vilas Memorial Sports Event provides a platform to raise awareness about cancer and to encourage people to get screened for cancer. The event also features educational talks and workshops on cancer prevention and detection.

BLOOD DONATION DRIVE

Blood donation is a vital part of healthcare, and it is essential to maintain a safe and adequate blood supply. Blood transfusions are used to treat a variety of conditions, including cancer, anemia, and trauma.
The Vilas Memorial Sports Event features a blood donation camp to encourage people to donate blood and save lives. The event also provides information about the blood donation process and the eligibility criteria for donating blood.

VILAS'24 INAUGURATION

On September 26, 2023, the Vilas Memorial Sports Event was inaugurated with a rally, slogans, and a speech by a professor on cancer awareness. The rally was led by students and faculty, who carried banners and placards with messages about the importance of sports and physical activity in preventing cancer. The slogans chanted by the participants included "Sports for Health, Health for Life" and "Cancer Awareness Through Sports." After the games, refreshments were served to the participants. The event was a great success, and it helped to raise awareness about the importance of sports and physical activity in preventing cancer
